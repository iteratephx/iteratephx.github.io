Speakers

Learn From These Great Folks

We are assembling a group of the strongest DevOps thought leaders in the Valley to share their thoughts on the state of the industry, where we are headed, and how we can get there.

Opening Keynote

  • Jason Hand

    DevOps Evangelist at VictorOps

    DevOps Evangelist at VictorOps, co-organizer of DevOpsDays - Rockies, author of ChatOps for Dummies, and host of a number of DevOps related meetups in the Denver, Boulder, and San Francisco areas.


    A DevOps Philosophy

    Throughout history, great philosophers, writers, and thinkers have passed along important thoughts and ideologies about our relationships, our passions, our purpose, and our place in this world.

    Time and again, they return to the idea of empathy in one form or another.

    Philosophy, like the DevOps movement, forces us to ask questions of ourselves, to empathize with each other, and to seek an understanding of our place in the bigger picture.

    In this talk, we will explore empathy, as well as additional common philosophical ideals and how they have helped shape the modern DevOps movement.

Topic Sessions

  • Maulik Shah

    Software Development Manager

    Maulik works as a Software Development Manager in the Valley and has been working on distributed systems for a while or two. He has a track record of taking systems and making them work smoothly.


    Building a DevOps Culture

    DevOps culture starts with motivation and is accelerated by tool sets. We’ll talk about specific tactics you can use to move your project, product, or team onto a path to a strong DevOps mentality.

  • Robyn Bergeron

    Community Architect at Ansible

    Robyn makes life awesome for folks in the Ansible community, growing its contributor ecosystem of development, advocacy, and best practices for Ansible and its related open source technologies and communities.


    The Democratization of DevOps

    Tools that "just work" are breaking down technical barriers and fostering new channels of communication — allowing rapid development and deployment by nearly anyone in an organization. But the continued growth of the DevOps movement depends on more than just tools — it also depends on a strong community of practice that embraces learning through successes and failures, and a shared commitment to continual improvement and evolution. The ability to improve your work life (and thus, your home life!) through practicing DevOps shouldn’t only be accessible to unicorns — and this talk will share how we can all participate in the democratization of DevOps.

  • Jeff Nickoloff

    geek, devops warrior, and software engineer

    Jeff digs micro-services, building products, breaking down big iron, and solving interesting problems. He is currently writing Docker In Action and running the Docker Phoenix Meetup.


    Containerize All the Things!

    This session will provide a 25 minute overview of containers and introduction to Docker. The next 20 minutes will be a lightning round. I'll build and publish Docker images for some of our favorite and least favorite tools and applications. I’ll cover best practices and how they relate to different Linux namespaces or features during the lightning round.

  • Will Button

    DevOps Engineer and Consultant in the valley

    Will has extensive experience working with tech startups to launch scalable, sustainable environments. In addition to working with Node, MongoDB, Elasticsearch, and AWS, he can often be found at @wfbutton and http://willbutton.co.


    No More Mr. Nice Guy - The MEAN Stack

    Nice engineers don't always finish last, but their websites do. Get MEAN and learn how to leverage the fault tolerance and scalability features of pm2, node, and mongodb to build MEAN stack apps that perform, scale and survive outages. Topics introduced will include clustering node using pm2 and data reliability with mongodb replica sets.

  • Sukrit Khera

    Pythonista, Groovist and Software Engineer

    Sukrit is a Senior Software Engineer and physics nerd at meltmedia in Tempe, AZ. He is the lead developer behind Totem, an open source continuous delivery and deployment framework.


    Continuous Delivery using GitFlow and Totem

    Learn about the key principles and patterns important in continuous delivery pipeline for micro-services. See these principles encoded and applied through the use of Git Flow and Totem, a continuous delivery platform for Dockerized micro-services.

  • Christian Posta

    Principal Middleware Architect, Red Hat

    Christian has worked for 10+ years helping teams build scalable, fault-tolerant distributed systems to solve business problems using Open Source solutions.


    The cold hard truth about microservices and how Open Source can help

    Microservice architectures have become popular, but we have to balance hype with reality. Microservices make it harder to manage deployments and create complex inter-service communications patterns. Learn how Open Source software built by open communities like Apache Camel, Docker, Kubernetes, OpenShift and Fabric8 can help achieve organizational goals to integrate services and establish effective continuous integration and continuous delivery pipelines.

  • Scott Delamater

    Staff Software Engineer, Shutterfly

    Scott has helped deliver the client applications responsible for over half of Shutterfly’s $1B annual revenue.


    Delighting Customers with Continuous Clients

    Continuous delivery is a full stack activity, and that includes you front-end geeks. In this session, we’ll walk through the delivery cycle of a client application, starting with a change on the developer’s machine and ending with a (live!) production deployment.

  • Colin Corstorphine

    Head of Corporate Sales Engineering, Sumo Logic

    In a variety of roles, Colin has been instrumental in helping Enterprise and SMB customers take data and transform it into actionable information at Sumo Logic and Merced Systems.


    Monitoring deployment and containerization tools

    The explosion of machine data and technologies to manage the growth has provided unique opportunities and challenges for the data professional. A strategy for monitoring and alerting on this data is crucial for ensuring that the noise is filtered and mission critical information is not overlooked.

Practical Sessions

  • Survey of Containerized Deployment Options

    Speaker: Josh Padnick

    First, we'll cover the pros and cons of the different container deployment options, e.g. CoreOS & Fleet, AWS EC2 Container Service, and Apache Mesos. Then, we'll discuss specific recommendations for different types of projects.

  • Fast-Track Your Dev Environment Management with Vagrant

    Justin James

    Come learn how Vagrant will make it easier to manage your development environment setup and remove the "works on my machine" excuse for your team. We will start with an overview of Vagrant and then dive into creating Vagrant machines. You will walk away with everything you need to have Vagrant manage your development environment.

  • Diagnose and Improve Front End Performance

    Speaker: Lukas Ruebbelke

    Learn the basic rules of building high-performance websites, how to diagnose problems with PageSpeed, and how to improve them with the Gulp toolchain.

  • Let's Encrypt!

    Speaker: Paul Shannon

    Use Let's Encrypt! to secure a site for free! Let's Encrypt is a new Certificate Authority that is free, automated, open, and backed by Mozilla, EFF, Cisco, and Akamai.
